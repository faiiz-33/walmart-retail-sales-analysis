# Dashboard 1: Sales Summary â€” Key Insights

### 1- Overall Performance
- **Total Sales:** ~$6.74B
- **Average Weekly Sales:** ~$15.98K
- **Coverage:** 45 stores and 81 departments

* Walmart generates strong and consistent weekly revenue across a large network of stores and departments.

---

### 2- Sales Trend Over Time
- **2011** recorded the highest total sales
- Sales in **2012 declined** compared to the 2011 peak

* Performance peaked during the post-recovery period before stabilizing.

---

### 3- Top 10 Departments by Weekly Sales
- A small number of departments dominate total sales
- Departments **92 and 95** are the top revenue contributors

* Sales are highly concentrated, suggesting targeted optimization opportunities.

---

### 4- AVG Weekly Sales by Store Type
- **Store Type A** accounts for the majority of total sales (~$20.1K)
- Store Types B and C contribute significantly less

* Larger or premium store formats drive most of the revenue.

---

### 5- Holiday vs Non-Holiday Performance
- **Average weekly sales are higher during holiday weeks**
- Holiday weeks outperform non-holiday weeks by approximately **7%**

* Holidays increase sales intensity per week, despite representing fewer weeks overall.

---

## Dashboard 2: External Factors â€” Key Insights

### 1- Consumer Price Index (CPI)
- CPI shows a **steady upward trend** from 2010 to 2012

* Indicates rising price levels during the analysis period.

---

### 2- Fuel Prices vs Average Weekly Sales
- Fuel prices fluctuate over time
- Average weekly sales remain relatively stable

* No clear relationship between fuel prices and sales at the aggregate level.

---

### 3- Unemployment Trend
- Unemployment shows a **clear downward trend** over time

* Improving labor conditions may support consumer spending, but no direct short-term sales impact is observed.

---

### 4- Store Size vs Average Weekly Sales
- Larger stores tend to generate higher average weekly sales
- Relationship is positive but not strictly linear

* Store size matters, but it is not the sole driver of performance.

---

##  Key Business Takeaways
- Sales are **highly concentrated** in specific stores and departments
- **Seasonality (holidays, December)** significantly boosts weekly sales
- External economic factors (fuel prices, CPI, unemployment) show **limited direct impact**
- Store characteristics influence performance more than macroeconomic variables
